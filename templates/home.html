About the Project

Malaria is one of the worldâ€™s most fatal infectious diseases, responsible for hundreds of thousands of deaths annually. It is a major public health issue in Africa, particularly affecting children under five and pregnant women. Traditional diagnosis methods, such as microscopic examination of blood slides, are time-consuming, require skilled technicians, and are often scarce in rural areas.

The goal of this project is to develop a multiclass object detection and classification model that can accurately identify and classify malaria parasites in blood slide images. The solution will address diagnostic challenges in Africa, aiding in the detection of malaria and differentiating between infected and uninfected blood cells.

Automated diagnostic solutions can improve healthcare efficiency, reduce burden on healthcare workers, and assist in large-scale screening programs, helping to detect and control malaria outbreaks.
